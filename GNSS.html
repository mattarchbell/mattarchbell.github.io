How I managed GNSS Error

Atmospheric Error

I chose to conduct my GNSS collection measurements on a clear day with a cool temperature of 9 degrees Celsius. Canada has a low TEC so that hopefully didn't affect my readings too much.

Satellite Geometry Error

I made my observations at around 3:45 PM, where Number of Satellites peaked for the day (fig 1.)

This time also - likely due to the number of satellites - had some of the lowest DOPs (fig 2.)

PDOP recorded on my phone was approximately 2.325 m on average over 4 readings. It was unable to be calculated for the readings I took that were averaged, but these averaged readings appear even more accurate

Multi-path Error

This error was tough to avoid. The survey point was behind a cedar tree next to a tall church tower… However I was outdoors, not inside, this probably helped a little.

Intended Error

Cannot avoid this inaccuracy on my phone. I am hoping that this issue doesn’t really exist too much anymore. However, my phone has a small antennae, so it likely doesn’t have the greatest receiver accuracy of all time…

Reciever Timing Error

This is not really something I can avoid. This along with atmospheric errors were likely the largest cause of GNSS location error during my observations.

Accuracy Estimate

I think my best results occurred when I averaged 25 points, however I had some very inaccurate results over the course of my testing. 2 back to back tests with 25 points very nearly the same result, while 2 back to back tests 2 minutes later yielded nearly the same result, but almost 6 meters away!

Unfortunately, my survey point was a vertical benchmark and the Location accuracy was way off - it was extrapolated quite a long time afterward from the position on the map. I included an estimated "True point" to show how inaccurate my results were. I don't think I had any better than 10m accuracy, even while averaging points.
