Matthew Archbell, B.Sc.

About Me

Current Studies

I am currently attending Fleming College for full time studies in the Geographic Information Systems - Applications Specialist postgraduate program in Lindsay. I Previously completed a Bachelors of Science Degree in the Wildlife Biology and Conservation program at the University of Guelph. My interests lie in using GIS and remote sensing in the realm of conservation to help shape future policy development.

Areas of Interest

When I'm not spending time on my studies, I enjoy spending time outdoors. I am an avid birder and often contribute wildlife sitings using the I-Naturalist app and E-Bird. I am also an active participant in the Citizen Science program Adopt a Pond, run by the Toronto zoo, and contribute data to both the Ontario Turtle Tally and Frogwatch conservation efforts. I like to use these opportunities as an excuse to get out for a hike or a paddle!

Recent Volunteer Positions

This past spring I had the opportunity to volunteer for nightjar surveys to help determine nightjar range in southern Ontario with The Land Between. I also volunteered with Blazing Star Environmental in partnership with the Canadian Wildlife Service and Trent University to monitor the Western Chorus Frog, a threatened species in Ontario (click hereto learn more about WCF monitoring).
